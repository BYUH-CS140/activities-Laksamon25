Chiang Mai

A lantern festival in Thailand is a breathtaking celebration of light, tradition, and spirituality. One of the most famous is Yi Peng, held in Chiang Mai. During this festival, thousands of glowing sky lanterns (called khom loi) are released into the night sky, symbolizing the letting go of bad luck and misfortune. People gather around temples and rivers dressed in traditional Thai clothing, making wishes as they release their lanterns.

At the same time, Loy Krathong is celebrated across Thailand, where people float beautifully decorated banana-leaf baskets (krathong) on rivers to pay respect to the water goddess and cleanse away negativity.
